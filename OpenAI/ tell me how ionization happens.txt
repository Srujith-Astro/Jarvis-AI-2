 Ionization is the process of adding or removing electrons from an atom or molecule, resulting in the formation of an ion. It can be caused by high energy collisions with other particles, such as when a beam of charged particles, such as electrons or protons, is fired at a target material, or by exposure to high energy radiation, such as X-rays or ultraviolet light. When a particle collides with an atom, it can dislodge an electron, leaving a positively charged ion. Similarly, when a high energy photon strikes an atom, it can impart enough energy to its electrons to cause them to escape the atom, leaving a negative ion.
